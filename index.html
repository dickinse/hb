Aeronaut History

The first of our kind.

  • December 2012

    Much Abrew About Nothing

    In a Brainstorming Session with Dr. Nandu, he suggests the names "Wombat Scrotum" and "Guitar Penis". For some reason, none of these stick, and the brewery opens under the name TBD Brewing.

  • July 2013

    Single-Barrel Homebrew Prototype #001

    Mike, Dan and Ronn are pleased to have completed TBD’s first prototype homebrew. TBD has its own wort! In a few weeks, we’ll have our first TBD beer: a session IPA, which will be fermented with two different strains of yeast and dry hopped with two different hops.

  • January 1 2014

    Happy Brew Year!

    As many will have noticed, TBD has been renamed AERONAUT Brewing Company. On the eve of 2014, we received word from the Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau (TTB) that we are approved for brewing.

  • June 2015

    Transition to Full Service

    AERONAUTS taproom becomes full service and adjust it's hours. We're now opne until 11pm! A Year with Dr. Nandu is released.

  • July 2014

    Aeronaut let's it's beaker flag fly

    To be fair, we’ve had a yeast lab all along, since day one, but it'd been a combination in the brew space. Now they've outgrown their space in the brewery, and have a lab to rival Dogfish Head's.

What this means to us

Your company has adopted an Open Door Policy for all employees. This means, literally, that every person's door is open to everyone. The purpose of our open door policy is to encourage open communication, feedback, and discussion about any matter of importance to an employee. Our open door policy means that everyone is free to talk with any person at any time about any topic.

What this means for you

If any area of your work is causing you concern, you have the responsibility to address your concern with an appropriate party.

Whether you have a problem, a complaint, a suggestion, or an observation, your company managers want to hear from you. By listening to you, the company is able to improve, to address complaints, and to foster employee understanding of the rationale for practices, processes, and decisions.
